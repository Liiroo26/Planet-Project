Pluto is a dwarf planet located in the Kuiper Belt, discovered by Clyde Tombaugh in 1930 and officially reclassified in 2006. It is characterized by its elliptical orbit that sometimes brings it closer to the Sun than Neptune. The planet's surface is covered in nitrogen ice and features the prominent heart-shaped feature known as Tombaugh Regio. New Horizons provided the first close-up images of Pluto in 2015, revealing a surprisingly complex and active geology. Pluto has five known moons, with its largest, Charon, being so massive relative to Pluto that they orbit a common center of gravity. The dwarf planet has a thin atmosphere that freezes and falls to the surface as it moves away from the Sun in its 248-year orbit. Despite its reclassification, Pluto remains scientifically significant as a plutoid and a key to understanding the early solar system.
